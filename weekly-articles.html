Week 01 / AI Foundations

How AI Is Changing Construction Workflows

Main idea: AI is beginning to support construction tasks such as document review, scheduling assistance, safety tracking, cost forecasting, and communication.

Reflection: My key takeaway is that AI is most useful when it supports human decision-making instead of replacing professional judgment. In construction, field knowledge and reliable data are still very important.

Week 02 / Data Quality

Good Data Comes Before Good AI

Main idea: AI tools depend on structured, accurate, and complete data. Poor data can create poor recommendations, even when the AI tool looks advanced.

Reflection: This connects directly to facilities management because asset names, equipment tags, O&M manuals, warranties, and CMMS records must be reliable before automation can truly help.

Week 03 / BIM and Digital Handover

Using BIM Beyond Construction Completion

Main idea: BIM should not only be treated as a design or construction tool. When prepared correctly, it can support maintenance, space planning, asset tracking, and long-term operations.

Reflection: A useful BIM handover needs technician-friendly navigation, accurate asset information, and clear links to maintenance documents. Otherwise, the model may not be used after turnover.

Week 04 / Responsible AI

Ethics, Risk, and Accountability in AI Use

Main idea: AI can improve productivity, but organizations must manage risks such as bias, privacy, hallucinated information, unclear accountability, and overreliance on automated outputs.

Reflection: In construction and FM, AI results should be reviewed by qualified people. A practical workflow should include checking sources, confirming field conditions, and documenting decisions.

Week 05 / AI Adoption

Why Some Companies Adopt AI Faster Than Others

Main idea: AI adoption is not only a technical issue. It also depends on leadership support, employee training, budget, data readiness, and trust in the tools.

Reflection: For construction companies, starting with small practical use cases may be better than trying to transform everything at once. Clear training and measurable benefits can help people accept change.

Week 06 / Future of FM

AI, Digital Twins, and Facility Lifecycle Management

Main idea: AI and digital twins can support future facilities management by connecting asset data, sensor trends, maintenance history, and building performance information.

Reflection: The biggest opportunity is creating a more connected workflow from design and construction to operations. The challenge is making sure the information is accurate, maintained, and useful for field staff.

Final Reflection

What these articles helped me understand.

Across the semester, the weekly articles helped me see that AI adoption in construction and facilities management is not just about new software. It requires clean data, realistic use cases, leadership support, training, and a strong connection to the people who actually use the information in the field.
